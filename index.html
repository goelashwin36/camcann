EXISTING PROBLEMS AND OUR SOLUTIONS

Revenue losses in stores

Problem

According to market survey, 49% of the revenue losses in retail stores is due to shoplifting. This excludes the internal theft, which also contributes with a weight age of 35% .


Solution

Tracking of movements as the basis for in-store and out-of-store analysis

When retailers can monitor customer traffic, including flows, timing, and even the stops they make, they can draw extremely valuable conclusions.

Customer retention

Problem

The current retail market doesn’t focus on retention of customers, which has a huge potential in itself to increase revenue.


Solution

Capture, manage, analyze and centrally provision customer data

Centralized customer data can help retailers build a seamless, fluid experience – beginning with an easily-accessible customer profile.

Inventory management

Problem

It’s not difficult to guess the inventory needed for popular goods. But for no-so-popular goods, inventory management is challenging but essential.


Solution

Stock accuracy

It is important to know how many products are present, which helps retailers to prevent out-of-stock, shrinkage and overstocks.

Customer analysis

Problem

Understanding what the customer wants by analyzing their behaviour is not something that our market has been focusing as of now.


Solution

Analyse for Better Decision-making

The real key to an outstanding experience is personalization. Proper analysis to develop effective marketing strategies according to consumer profiling can increase the sales in stores.

Long billing counter

Problem

Long billing counters is also a big issue, which does not drive the customers into stores and hence reduces sales.


Solution

Avoid long billing counters

Auto-detection of products into the system after the customer plans to buy it which shall help in faster payments.
